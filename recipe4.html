Harees

Back to Home | Back to Arabic Cuisine

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups Wheat grains (or Harees wheat, soaked overnight)
  • 500g Chicken (cut into pieces or use boneless chicken)
  • 1 large Onion (finely chopped)
  • 4 Garlic cloves (minced)
  • 2 tbsp Ghee or Butter
  • 1/2 tsp Ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp Ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp Ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups Chicken broth (or water)
  • 1/2 tsp Ground cardamom (optional)
  • Chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)
  • Fried onions (for garnish, optional)

Preparation Time

1 hour 30 minutes

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the ghee or butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken pieces and brown them for about 5 minutes on all sides.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth (or water) and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, lower the heat to a simmer.
  5. Add the soaked wheat, turmeric, cinnamon, black pepper, and salt. Stir everything together, making sure the wheat is submerged in the liquid.
  6. Cover the pot and cook on low heat for about 1 hour, or until the wheat is soft and the chicken is tender. Stir occasionally to prevent the wheat from sticking to the bottom of the pot.
  7. Once the wheat has broken down and the consistency is thick, remove the chicken pieces from the pot. Shred the chicken and return it to the pot.
  8. Continue to cook for an additional 15-20 minutes until everything is well combined and the mixture reaches a smooth, creamy consistency.
  9. Serve the harees hot, garnished with fresh parsley and fried onions if desired.
